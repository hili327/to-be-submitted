Understanding Age-Related Diseases

Age-related diseases vary depending on the stage of life. From childhood illnesses to conditions that affect the elderly, understanding these diseases helps in early diagnosis and better management.

Childhood Diseases

Common illnesses affecting children, such as measles, chickenpox, and asthma.

Adulthood Diseases

Conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and certain cancers prevalent in adulthood.

Elderly Diseases

Diseases like Alzheimer’s, osteoporosis, and arthritis that commonly affect the elderly.

Childhood Diseases

Measles: A viral infection causing fever, cough, and a distinctive rash.

Chickenpox: A highly contagious disease marked by itchy blisters all over the body.

Asthma: A respiratory condition causing difficulty in breathing due to inflamed airways.

Adulthood Diseases

Diabetes: A chronic condition that affects the way the body processes blood sugar.

Hypertension: High blood pressure, often called a silent killer.

Cancer: A group of diseases characterized by abnormal cell growth.

Elderly Diseases

Alzheimer's Disease: A progressive condition that destroys memory and cognitive function.

Osteoporosis: A disease where bones become brittle and fragile.

Arthritis: Inflammation of joints causing pain and stiffness.
